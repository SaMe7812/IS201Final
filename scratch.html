My Scratch Page: So Many Cool Guitarists

Here is a page that I designed all by myself. Is it the prettiest? No. But does it work? Yes. And that is what matters.

Here is a list with some of the coolest guitarists ever and some fun facts about them all according to chat.

Guitar: Only the coolest instrument EVER. There are so many types of guitar too. I used to get the Taylor Guitar magazine and just look at it forever wishing I had enough money to buy one. Someday.

  1. Jimi Hendrix:

    Revolutionized electric guitar with distortion, feedback, left-handed playing, and wild creativity.
    • Played a right-handed guitar flipped upside down because he was left-handed.
    • Before fame, he played backup for Little Richard and The Isley Brothers
    • Burned his guitar on stage at the 1967 Monterey Pop Festival as part of an improvised ceremony.

  2. Eric Clapton:

    Blues-rock legend known for smooth tone, emotion, and pure technical mastery.
    • The only musician inducted into the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame 3 times
    • Fans used to graffiti “Clapton is God” around London in the 60s.

  3. Jimmy Page (Led Zeppelin):

    • Played guitar with a violin bow during concerts (“Dazed and Confused”).
    • Was one of London's top session guitarists before Led Zeppelin (he played on over 50 hit records!).
    • His double-neck guitar was built so he could play both 6-string and 12-string parts live in “Stairway to Heaven.”

  4. Eddie Van Halen:

    Innovator of tapping, harmonics, and fast-but-melodic playing.
    • Invented the famous two-handed tapping technique (popularized in “Eruption”).
    • Built his own guitar—the Frankenstrat—from mismatched parts.
    • Played the “Beat It” guitar solo for Michael Jackson as a favor (for free!).

  5. B.B. King:

    The King of Blues. His vibrato and phrasing influenced almost every guitarist after him.
    • Named all his guitars “Lucille.”
    • Got the name after rescuing his guitar from a bar fire that started over a fight between two men—over a girl named Lucille.
    • Once averaged 300 concerts a year for decades.
